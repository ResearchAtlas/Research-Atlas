1. Executive Summary
This compendium serves as a critical strategic asset for modern research teams navigating the "Paradigm of Augmented Inquiry." In an era characterized by exponential scholarly output and the "Snoopy Problem" of information saturation, traditional manual synthesis is no longer a viable competitive strategy. This report provides a structured framework to transition from "vibe-based" prompting to rigorous Prompt Engineering Architectures, enabling researchers to amplify cognitive capacity while maintaining absolute fidelity to empirical rigor and ethical standards. By deploying the verified assets contained herein, research teams can reduce time-to-insight by 40–70%, transforming the Large Language Model (LLM) from a simple chatbot into a high-fidelity reasoning engine.
Key Strategic Takeaways
Architectural Prompting: High-fidelity research outputs require structured frameworks (TIDD-EC for implementation, COSTAR for conceptualization) rather than simple natural language queries.
The Verification Imperative: AI is a pattern predictor, not a fact-retrieval engine; 40% of AI-generated citations are fabricated, necessitating the mandatory implementation of the VERIFY framework.
Deterministic Precision: Controlling technical parameters like Temperature, Top-P, and Top-K is essential to ensuring factual accuracy in statistical and technical reporting.
Reproducibility Standards: AI-assisted analysis must be integrated into "Literate Programming" environments (e.g., R Markdown) to maintain a verifiable and auditable research trail.
Ethical Accountability: Major publishers (Elsevier, Nature, Wiley) prohibit AI authorship; human researchers retain sole legal and scientific responsibility for all AI-supported content.

3. Prompt Catalog
3.1 How to Use This Catalog
Transitioning from "vibe-based" queries to "Prompt Engineering Architectures" is required for professional research. Prompts in this catalog are tagged using two primary frameworks derived from Source 07 and Source 09:
COSTAR (Context, Objective, Style, Tone, Audience, Response): Used for conceptual tasks, drafting, and role-based simulations.
TIDD-EC (Task, Intent, Data, Domain, Explicit Constraints): Used for implementation, coding, and statistical analysis where precise constraints are mandatory.
When choosing a prompt, identify the phase of your research. Use COSTAR-based prompts for Conceptualization and Literature Synthesis to ensure appropriate tone. Use TIDD-EC-based prompts for Data Analysis and Technical Editing to prevent hallucination through strict constraints.

3.3 Prompt Cards (Detailed)
Card: P-01 Hybrid Statistical Reasoning
Purpose: Enforces assumption checking (normality, equal variance) before executing statistical tests to ensure methodological rigor.
Best Fit Scenarios: Medical research, experimental validation, clinical trial reporting.
Inputs Required: Raw dataset or descriptive summary, target variables, research question.
Output Format: Step-by-step reasoning log, selected test name, test statistics (t, F, etc.), and p-value.
Quality Checklist:
Check for normality (Shapiro-Wilk)?
Check for homogeneity of variance (Levene’s)?
Alternative test (e.g., Mann-Whitney) selected if assumptions fail?
Safety/Ethics Notes: Results must be verified by a human statistician; AI can misinterpret outliers as significant data points.
Source Pointer: SRC-08.
Prompt Text Verbatim:
First, check whether the data meet the assumptions for a t-test (normality and equal variances). 
If the assumptions are met, perform the t-test. If not, select and perform an appropriate 
alternative test. Report the results, including the test statistic, degrees of freedom, and p-value.

Purpose: Adopts a high-level expert persona to subject a manuscript to a "simulated peer review."
Best Fit Scenarios: Finalizing PhD chapters or conference submissions (ICML/ICLR).
Inputs Required: Draft manuscript (PDF or LaTeX text), target submission venue.
Output Format: Part 1 [Review Report], Part 2 [Strategic Action Plan].
Quality Checklist:
Identify hidden logical leaps.
Check alignment between intro and conclusion.
Flag missing baselines.
Safety/Ethics Notes: Ensure PII (Personally Identifiable Information) is redacted.

4.2 Workflow Writeups
The Reproducible Data-to-Visualization Pipeline
Objective: To move from raw data to a publication-ready chart while maintaining a strict audit trail.
Strategic Impact: Ensures every visualization is grounded in a verified statistical assessment, reducing the risk of misleading graphical representations.
Step-by-Step Execution Guide:
Step 1: Initial Assessment. Use Prompt P-04. Identify data types and hygiene issues.
Step 2: Cleaning. Direct the LLM to handle missing values and standardize formats (dates, numbers).
Step 3: Statistical Check. Use Prompt P-01 to confirm the distribution allows for the intended visualization.
Step 4: Visual Specification. Define chart type, X/Y axes (ensuring Y starts at zero), and series colors (using colorblind-accessible palettes).
So What? This sequence prevents "Vibe Coding"—generating pretty charts with no statistical basis. By using R Markdown within this workflow, the researcher creates an auditable "Literate Programming" trail.
The "Augmented Inquiry" Drafting Loop
Objective: To iterate through sections of a manuscript while preserving authorial voice and logical rigor.
Strategic Impact: Minimizes the risk of "AI-flavor" and structural drift across long manuscripts (e.g., 70-page chapters).
Step-by-Step Execution Guide:
Step 1: Skeleton Generation. Create an outline based on the research question.
Step 2: Iterative Drafting. Draft sections one-by-one, providing the LLM with the "Seed" evidence for each.
Step 3: Logic Audit. Apply Prompt P-02 to the completed section to identify unsupported claims.
Step 4: Technical Polish. Apply Prompt P-03 to finalize the LaTeX formatting and academic tone.
So What? By breaking the manuscript into discrete units and subjecting each to a logical audit before polishing, the researcher ensures the final product is cohesive and resistant to desk rejection.

5. Educational Materials: Using AI in Research
5.1 The Capabilities and Limitations of Research AI
AI is a "supportive tool" rather than a primary author. Major publishers emphasize that while AI can assist in synthesis, it cannot take legal responsibility for the integrity of a study.
AI Capabilities
AI Limits
Pattern Generation: Identifying trends in large datasets.
Pattern Prediction vs. Fact: AI predicts likely words; it does not "know" facts.
Summarization: Condensing 70-page chapters into abstracts.
Lack of Responsibility: AI cannot be held legally liable for scientific errors.
Language Polish: Enhancing fluency for ESL writers.
Hallucinations: 40% risk of fabricated citations in raw queries.
Code Scripting: Generating R/Python scripts.
PII Risks: Sensitive data uploaded to public LLMs may violate IP laws.

5.2 Prompting Fundamentals for Researchers
Chain-of-Thought (CoT): Adding "Let's think step by step" to improve reasoning in complex logic.
Temperature Settings:
Low (0–0.2): Factual, precise. Use for statistical reporting and data analysis.
High (0.8–1.0): Creative, random. Use for brainstorming only.
Top-P (Nucleus Sampling): Limits the cumulative probability of next-token candidates. Lower Top-P (e.g., 0.1) ensures more deterministic, factual responses.
Top-K: Restricts the model to the top K most likely next words. Essential for technical tasks to prevent "wandering" logic.
5.3 Verification and Evaluation (The VERIFY Framework)
Implement the VERIFY heuristic to spot suspicious AI content:
V: Vague Matches. Does the info match the prompt without nuanced detail?
E: Excessive Specificity. Are numbers suspiciously convenient or detailed?
R: Recent Claims. Does the AI cite papers from after its training cutoff date?
The Three-Layer Protocol (Source 14):
Quick (2-5 mins): Verify DOIs in CrossRef to ensure the citation exists.
Detailed (10-20 mins): Fact-check core claims against established textbooks or PDFs.
Expert: Consult a domain specialist or senior researcher for controversial interpretations.
5.4 Ethics, Privacy, and Human Subjects
Publisher policies (Elsevier, Springer, Wiley) are unanimous: AI cannot be listed as an author.
Disclosure: Any use of AI for content generation must be disclosed in the Methods or Acknowledgements.
Pre-Submission Checklist:
Have I redacted Personally Identifiable Information (PII)?
Is my data embargoed?
Have I verified all AI-generated citations?

Appendix C: Glossary
RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation): Connecting an LLM to a specific external database (e.g., PubMed) to reduce hallucinations by grounding the output in real-world text.
Temperature: A setting (0–1.0) controlling randomness; lower values are more factual and consistent.
Hallucination: When an AI generates factually incorrect information that appears semantically plausible.
Semantic Search: Searching by meaning and conceptual intent rather than simple keyword matching.
Zero-shot: Requesting a task without providing any examples of the desired output.
Chain-of-Thought (CoT): A technique where the model is asked to "think step by step" to improve logical reasoning.
